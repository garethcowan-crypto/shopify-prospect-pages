What We Heard from Aaron
Key takeaways from our initial discovery conversation on May 6.
🥔

Family-Owned Since 1958

Founded by Joyce and Paul Herlitzke. Now led by Scott (President) and Aaron (VP Finance & Operations). 150+ employees, 100+ tractor-trailers, serving grocery retailers across the Upper Midwest.

📦

Pure B2B Wholesale Produce

1,000+ fresh produce items and complementary grocery products — fruits, vegetables, salad dressings, croutons, and more. Own fleet picks up from West Coast and Northwest, breaks it down, and distributes to grocery stores.

📞

Phone-First Ordering Today

The vast majority of orders come in by phone. Buyers call, discuss product quality ('how do the cherries look today?'), and build orders on the fly. Many won't sit down and retype into a portal — they prefer calling it in.

🌙

After-Hours & Saturday Pain

When Potato King is closed, buyers can't add to their orders. Sales people come in Saturday mornings so customers can call in weekend add-ons. Aaron wants to eliminate this — let buyers log in on their phone and add items after hours.

❌

Consentio Failed

Previously tried Consentio (Spain-based API for Dynamics). Too much downtime, customers got frustrated, and support was 24 hours away due to time zones. Dismantled two months ago. 'Bad taste in our mouth' — need reliability this time.

💻

Microsoft Dynamics 365

Everything lives in Dynamics — pricing, customers, invoicing, terms. Aaron needs the portal tightly integrated so orders flow straight into Dynamics as posted sales invoices on the customer's existing payment terms. Not paying at checkout.

ERP Integration
Shopify + Microsoft Dynamics 365
A native connection — not a custom API from Spain.

Shopify has a pre-built connector for Microsoft Dynamics 365 — one of the most common ERPs in the wholesale distribution space. Here's how data flows:

  • Pricing and customer groups sync from Dynamics → Shopify automatically
  • Inventory levels update in real time across your product catalog
  • Orders placed on Shopify push back into Dynamics as posted sales invoices
  • Customer payment terms (Net 30/60/90) are respected — no checkout payment required
  • Product catalog and item names pulled directly from Dynamics
  • Changes in Dynamics reflect in Shopify without manual intervention

Unlike Consentio, this is a supported, maintained connector — not a custom API built by a team in another time zone.
