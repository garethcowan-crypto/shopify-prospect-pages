DEMO SUMMARY
What We Showed You
Key capabilities demonstrated on the call — mapped to The Potato King's specific use case.
🔐

Gated B2B Portal

Your storefront is completely locked. No products, no pricing visible until a customer logs in. Passwordless two-factor authentication makes it simple for buyers to access — no passwords to remember or write down.

💰

Customer-Specific Pricing

Every customer group sees their own contracted pricing, pulled directly from Dynamics. Festival Foods sees their rates, mom-and-pop stores see theirs. Pricing tables sync from Dynamics daily, with the ability to push emergency changes instantly.

📱

After-Hours Mobile Ordering

The core use case: a produce manager at 1am needs 5 more cases of lettuce for tomorrow. They open their phone, log in, add items, and submit. No phone call needed. No Saturday morning staff required. Order ships next day.

📋

Quick Order & Reorder

Buyers can search by SKU, use a CSV upload for bulk orders, or hit 'buy again' on previous orders. Since 90% of items are the same order-to-order, reordering takes seconds — just adjust quantities and submit.

🧾

No Payment at Checkout

'Choose payment method later' — orders flow through as invoices on existing terms (Net 30). No credit card required. Produce managers place orders, the mothership accounting team pays by check once a month. Exactly how you operate today.

📊

Inventory Visibility

Real-time inventory from Dynamics. We configured it to allow negative inventory so you never miss a sale — if stock hits zero, orders still go through and alert your team to source more. Customer-facing inventory counts can be hidden.

👥

Multi-Location Accounts

Super users can switch between multiple store locations within the portal. Permissions control what each user can see and do — a produce manager sees different options than an account administrator.

⚙️

Click-Not-Code Admin

Aaron builds it, then hands it off. The admin interface is fully declarative — no coding needed. Products, companies, permissions, and settings are all managed through a visual interface. Bulk import/export via spreadsheet or ERP sync.

How Does This Apply to The Potato King?

Your revenue is $70-80M, but the key question is: how much will flow through Shopify?

If you start with the snowball approach (limited catalog, after-hours add-ons only):

  • Year 1 Shopify GMV estimate: $2-5M
  • Monthly GMV: $167K-$417K
  • VPF check: $417K × 0.18% = $750 → below $2,300 → flat $2,300/mo
  • Annual platform cost: $27,600

If adoption grows and more volume flows through Shopify:

  • At $10M/yr ($833K/mo): $833K × 0.18% = $1,500 → still flat $2,300/mo
  • At $15M/yr ($1.25M/mo): $1.25M × 0.18% = $2,250 → still flat $2,300/mo
  • At $20M/yr ($1.67M/mo): $1.67M × 0.18% = $3,006 → VPF applies (+$706/mo)

Since you're not processing payments on Shopify (all check/wire → Dynamics), there are zero transaction processing fees on top of the platform fee.

Important: The VPF applies to orders that originate in Shopify. We're confirming the exact treatment for orders where payment is collected outside the platform (check/wire into Dynamics). Gareth will follow up with specifics.

ERP INTEGRATION
Shopify + Microsoft Dynamics 365
Your source of truth stays in Dynamics. Shopify surfaces it to your buyers.

Shopify has a native, pre-built connector for Microsoft Dynamics 365. Aaron noted that Dynamics already comes with Shopify compatibility built in — reducing concerns about API reliability after the Consentio experience.

  • Customer pricing tables sync from Dynamics → Shopify (daily sync + manual push for emergencies)
  • Inventory levels update in real time — negative inventory supported
  • Orders placed on Shopify flow back into Dynamics as new invoices
  • Product catalog (800+ stocked items) imported via ERP sync or spreadsheet
  • Product images stored in Shopify (Aaron has the image database from Consentio)
  • No separate database to maintain — Dynamics remains the single source of truth

Aaron's Dynamics reseller (Infotech) may be able to support the Shopify integration. If Infotech doesn't have a Shopify practice, Gareth will recommend an implementation partner.
