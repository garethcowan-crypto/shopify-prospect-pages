Decision Framework
When D2C Merchants Choose Plus Over Advanced
It's not about size alone. These are the specific triggers that make Plus the right call.
Plus trigger

You Need Checkout Customization

Custom checkout fields, post-purchase upsells, branded checkout experience, conditional logic based on cart contents or customer segment. If the default checkout isn't enough, you need Plus.

Plus trigger

You're Running Multiple Brands or Markets

ORLY has 20+ brands. If any of them need their own storefront — different branding, different domain, different market — expansion stores are Plus-only. Up to 9 included.

Plus trigger

You Want to Reduce Ad Spend Waste

Shopify Audiences uses first-party data from Shop Pay's 100M+ buyers to build high-intent audiences for Meta, Google, and TikTok. Merchants see up to 50% lower CAC. Plus-only.

Plus trigger

You Need Automation at Scale

Full Shopify Flow lets you automate inventory alerts, fraud flagging, customer tagging, loyalty workflows, and order routing. Advanced has basic automation — Plus unlocks everything.

Plus trigger

You're Doing $1M+ and Transaction Fees Matter

On $2M in annual online revenue, the difference between 2.4% and 2.15% saves ~$5,000/yr in processing fees alone. That offsets a significant portion of the Plus cost.

Plus trigger

You May Add Wholesale Later

If the D2C brand succeeds and you want to add a wholesale channel, Plus gives you full B2B with 10,000 catalogs, company accounts, and payment terms. Advanced limits you to 3 catalogs.

Advanced is fine

You're Testing the Market First

If this is a lean launch to validate demand before scaling — small catalog, limited SKUs, no integrations — Advanced at $299/mo is a lower-risk starting point. You can upgrade to Plus later.

Advanced is fine

Simple Storefront, No Customization Needed

If a standard Shopify theme with default checkout, basic discounts, and standard reporting meets all your needs, Advanced delivers a full-featured D2C experience without the Plus price tag.

The Bottom Line for ORLY

If you're launching a D2C brand with plans to scale, run product drops or flash sales, customize the checkout, target high-intent buyers with Shopify Audiences, and potentially expand to multiple brand storefronts — Plus pays for itself. If you're testing the waters with a single brand and simple setup, Advanced is a solid starting point with a clear upgrade path.
